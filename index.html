Datamatch is a long-running Harvard tradition (80% student participation) that matches students on Valentines' Day based on survey responses. This year I spearheaded Datamatch's expansion to other schools, found consensus on policy to make Datamatch inclusive of nonbinary students, negotiated deals with local restuarants, and got the group publicity from top journalistic outlets. Last year I founded and lead a new data visualization subteam that produced D3 visualizations of cool aspects of Datamatch. Next year I will co-lead the whole project with Teddy Liu.
